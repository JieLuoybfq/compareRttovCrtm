Instrument:  IRS 

Instrument details
Acronym IRS
Full name Infra Red Sounder
Purpose Temperature/humidity sounding and wind profile derivation by tracking water vapour features
Short description Interferometer with large detector arrays for simultaneous sounding of more pixels [see detailed characteristics below]
Background New development
Scanning Technique The disk is split in 4 latitude belts, and is covered at 6-hour intervals; but not all at the same time. The northermost belt, that includes Europe, is imaged at 30-min intervals for the whole 6-hour period (12 images). The following belt is covered simultaneously with the first one, but the 30-min sequence is limited to 5 images. The third belt is covered after the second, by a sequence of 4 images. The southernmost belt is covered after the third, by only 3 images (the minimum to track a water-vapour patch)
Resolution 4.0 km at s.s.p.
Coverage / Cycle 30 min for the northermost 1/4 of the disk (that includes the European area). 6 hours for the whole disk
Mass 400 kg Power 750 W Data Rate

 

Providing Agency ESA
Instrument Maturity Demonstrated by simulations
Utilization Period: ≥2023 to ≥2039
Last update: 2019-08-14
